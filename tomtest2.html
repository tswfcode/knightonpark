Knighton Park

A 78 acre park situated only 15 minutes from Leicesters' city centre

Parks

This beautiful and popular 78 acre park has colourful shrub borders, pond, rock garden, two well-equipped play areas and a nine-hole pitch and putt golf course which is open during the holidays. Further information Trees are a strong feature of the park with avenues of laburnum close to the Spinney, gingko (maidenhair) avenue in the Heath Garden and a charming path bordered by magnolias across the middle of the park. In addition to the Spinney there are newly planted lime woods, a beech wood and willow wood.

Knighton Park History

Knighton village appears in the Domesday Book under its Scandinavian name of Cnihetone. In the 1720’s, Edmund Cradock, a woollen draper of Leicester bought Knighton Hall and its associated farmland. In the years that followed, the family acquired almost all the land in the parish. Knighton Hall is now the official residence of the Vice Chancellor of Leicester University and the name of the Cradock’s is preserved in the partially thatched Cradock pub. The City Council bought land from the Cradock-Hartopp trustees just before the Second World War with the intention of establishing a park but work was not begun until 1953.
